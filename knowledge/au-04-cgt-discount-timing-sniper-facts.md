## The Rule

**Name:** CGT Discount — 50% / 33.33% / 0% Timing Trap
**Short form:** Hold an investment asset for AT LEAST 12 months before the CGT event and you halve your taxable gain (50% discount for individuals and trusts). Sell at 11 months and 29 days and you pay tax on the full gain. The clock runs from contract DATE to contract DATE — not from settlement, not from possession. Companies get NO discount. Super funds get 33.33%, not 50%.
**Operative law:** Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997), Division 115
- s.115-10 — General CGT discount rule (50% for individuals/trusts, 33.33% for complying super funds)
- s.115-25 — 12-month ownership requirement
- s.115-100 — Discount percentages
- s.115-105 — Foreign resident apportionment (since 8 May 2012)
- s.115-215 — Trust distributions and CGT discount streaming
- s.104-10 — CGT event A1 (disposal date = contract date, not settlement)
**ATO landing page:** https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/capital-gains-tax/cgt-discount

## The Threshold

**Effective date:** 50% CGT discount for individuals introduced 21 September 1999 (replaced indexation method). 33.33% for super funds applied since the same date.
**Holding period:** AT LEAST 12 months between acquisition date and CGT event date. ATO excludes BOTH the day of acquisition AND the day of the CGT event from the count.
**Discount percentages by entity type:**
- **Individuals (Australian residents):** 50%
- **Trusts:** 50% (applied at trust level before distribution to beneficiaries)
- **Complying superannuation funds (including SMSFs):** 33.33% (one-third)
- **Eligible life insurance companies:** 33.33%
- **Companies:** 0% — no discount available
- **Foreign residents (post 8 May 2012 gains):** 0% — no discount on the post-2012 portion of the gain
- **Affordable rental housing (additional discount):** Up to 10% extra (so up to 60% for individuals/trusts) — ITAA 1997 s.115-115 added 12 December 2019, requires 3+ years (1,095 days) of qualifying use after 1 January 2018
**12-month clock:** Acquisition day excluded, CGT event day excluded. Asset held 11 months 29 days = NO discount. Asset held exactly 12 months = NO discount (technically). Asset held 12 months + 1 day = discount available.
**Contract-date rule:** CGT event date is the contract DATE for property sales (s.104-10), NOT settlement date. A property bought 4 June 2024 (contract) and sold 6 July 2025 (contract) qualifies. Settlement dates irrelevant.

## The Fear Number

**Gary's worked example: $33,300 lost discount on a one-week-too-early sale.**

Gary bought an investment property in Joondalup WA on 15 March 2024 (contract date) for $620,000. He sold it on 8 March 2025 (contract date) for $740,000.

- Capital gain: $120,000
- Days held (excluding both endpoints): 16 March 2024 to 7 March 2025 = 357 days
- Required: at least 366 days (12 months + 1 day)
- Result: SHORT BY 9 DAYS
- 50% CGT discount: NOT AVAILABLE
- Full $120,000 added to assessable income at marginal rate
- Tax at Gary's 37% marginal rate (incl. Medicare): **$44,400**

If Gary had waited 9 more days and signed contracts on 17 March 2025:
- Days held: 366
- 50% CGT discount: AVAILABLE
- Taxable portion: $120,000 × 50% = $60,000
- Tax at 37%: **$22,200**
- **Saved: $22,200 — but the spread between actual and best case is $44,400 - $22,200 = $22,200, plus the additional Medicare on the discount-eligible amount ≈ $33,300 to $44,400 cost of timing the sale wrong.**

Even simpler: $11,000 of avoidable tax for every week of timing error on a $120k gain.

## Who It Applies To

- Australian-resident individuals selling investment assets (property, shares, crypto, business assets, collectibles)
- Australian-resident trusts (the discount is applied at trust level before streaming gains to beneficiaries)
- Complying superannuation funds and SMSFs (at the reduced 33.33% rate)
- Eligible life insurance companies (at the reduced 33.33% rate)
- Owners of affordable rental housing (additional 10% — total up to 60%)
- Does NOT apply to companies (full gain assessable at 25%/30% company rate)
- Does NOT apply to foreign residents on post-8 May 2012 gains (apportioned exclusion)
- Does NOT apply to assets held less than 12 months
- Does NOT apply to certain CGT events that create new assets (restrictive covenants, granting leases, granting options)

## The Form

**Tax return reporting:** Capital gains worksheet within the individual income tax return for the financial year of the contract sale date.
**Form name:** Question 18 (supplementary section) on individual return — labels A and H for capital gain/discount.
**Trust return:** Capital gain reported at trust level, discount applied, then distributed to beneficiaries (s.115-215 streaming rules apply).
**Super fund return:** Capital gain reported in SMSF annual return, 33.33% discount applied if asset held > 12 months.
**ATO calculator:** ATO CGT calculator and record-keeping tool (online).
**Apply via:** myTax (online lodgement), tax agent, or super fund administrator.
**Required records:** Original purchase contract (showing acquisition date), sale contract (showing CGT event date), cost base components (stamp duty, legal fees, capital improvements, ownership costs not previously deducted), evidence of Australian residency throughout holding period.

## The Consequence

If you sell EXACTLY at or before 12 months:
- No CGT discount
- Full capital gain (less capital losses) assessable at marginal rate
- For a $120k gain at 37%: $44,400 tax
- For a $200k gain at 47%: $94,000 tax

If you sell AFTER 12 months (12 months + 1 day or later):
- Individual: 50% discount applied → $60k of $120k gain assessable → $22,200 tax at 37%
- Super fund: 33.33% discount applied → $80k of $120k gain assessable → tax at super rate (15% in accumulation, 0% in pension phase ECPI)
- Trust: 50% discount applied at trust level → distributed gain to beneficiaries already discounted

**Net effect:** A 1-day timing error can cost up to ~37-47% of the gross gain. A multi-week premature sale can be tens of thousands of dollars in unnecessary tax.

## Key Legal Caveats

1. **Foreign residents excluded post 8 May 2012.** If you became a foreign resident before the CGT event, the post-2012 portion of the gain attracts no discount. Apportionment is time-based: days as Australian resident vs days as foreign resident across the holding period.

2. **Pre-CGT cutoff.** Assets acquired BEFORE 11:45am AEST on 21 September 1999 can use either the indexation method OR the discount method — choose whichever gives the lower assessable gain. Most cases the discount wins, but offsetting capital losses can make indexation the better choice.

3. **Companies excluded entirely.** No CGT discount for any company structure. A capital gain in a company pays tax at 25% (base rate entity) or 30% (others) on the FULL gain. The CGT discount through trusts/individuals is a key reason structuring matters.

4. **Inherited assets and the 12-month rule.** When inheriting, the deceased's holding period counts toward your 12-month clock. An asset inherited after 6 years counts as already qualifying — you can sell immediately and still get the discount.

5. **"Home first used to produce income" interaction (s.118-192).** If your former main residence is sold within 12 months of when you first started renting it out, the CGT discount cannot be applied. The s.118-192 cost base reset combined with the 12-month rule can deny discounts on otherwise-qualifying former homes.

## The Deadline

**No fixed external deadline** — the trap activates at the timing of the CGT event. The CGT event date is the contract sale date (s.104-10), NOT settlement.

**For lodgement:**
- Capital gain reportable in the financial year of the contract date
- Standard ATO lodgement deadlines: 31 October if self-lodging, later via registered tax agent

**Strategic timing:**
- Plan sale contracts to fall AT LEAST 12 months + 1 day after acquisition contract
- Bridging financing can save the 12-month deadline if buyer is ready early
- Even 1 week of delay on signing can convert a $120k gain from $44,400 tax to $22,200 tax for an individual at 37% marginal rate

## Common Misconceptions

**MYTH:** "I held it for 12 months so I get the discount."
**FACT:** 12 months EXACTLY isn't enough. Need 12 months + 1 day. ATO excludes both the acquisition day AND the CGT event day from the count. Asset held precisely 365 days = no discount.

**MYTH:** "Settlement date is what counts for the 12-month clock."
**FACT:** CONTRACT date is the CGT event date for property sales (s.104-10). A property bought via contract on 1 January and settled on 15 February counts the 12-month clock from 1 January.

**MYTH:** "My company gets the 50% discount on its property sale."
**FACT:** Companies do not qualify for the CGT discount under any circumstance. Full gain is assessable at company tax rate (25%/30%).

**MYTH:** "My SMSF gets the same 50% discount as I would personally."
**FACT:** Complying super funds (including SMSFs) get 33.33%, not 50%. Two-thirds of the gain is included in fund income (taxed at 15% in accumulation, 0% in pension phase under ECPI).

**MYTH:** "Capital losses are applied AFTER the 50% discount."
**FACT:** Capital losses are applied BEFORE the discount. Apply current and carry-forward losses first, then halve the remaining gain. ATO calculator handles this; manual calculations often get this wrong.

## The Citation Gap

They miss the entity-specific rates: 50% for individuals/trusts, 33.33% for super funds, 0% for companies. They miss the apportionment rules for foreign residents post-8 May 2012.
