## The Rule
Name: Foreign Resident Capital Gains Withholding
Short form: FRCGW
Operative law: Taxation Administration Act 1953
  Schedule 1, Subdivision 14-D — "Capital proceeds involving foreign residents and taxable Australian property"
  Section 14-200 — withholding obligation (purchaser must pay the Commissioner an amount on acquiring CGT asset that is taxable Australian real property from a foreign resident)
  Section 14-210 — foreign-resident determination rules
  Section 14-215 — excluded transactions
  Section 14-220 — clearance certificate (Commissioner issues this; confirms vendor is Australian resident → no withholding required)
  Section 14-225 — entity declarations
  Section 14-235 — variations (Commissioner can vary the amount payable — this is the foreign-resident "variation certificate" path)
2025 change via: Treasury Laws Amendment (2024 Tax and Other Measures No. 1) Act 2024 (Act No. 135 of 2024), Royal Assent 10 December 2024, commencing 1 January 2025. The amending Act increased the withholding rate from 12.5% to 15% and reduced the threshold from $750,000 to $0.
ATO page: ato.gov.au/individuals/capital-gains-tax/property-and-capital-gains-tax/foreign-residents-and-cgt/

## The Threshold
Effective date: 1 January 2025 (applies to contracts entered on or after this date)
Threshold: $0 — all property sales captured
Rate: 15% of contract (sale) price
Base: contract price NOT profit
Previous threshold: $750,000 (changed by Treasury Laws Amendment 2024)

## The Fear Number (Gary's product)
Property value $900,000:
  Withholding = $135,000 (15% × $900,000)

Property value $1,000,000:
  Withholding = $150,000

Property value $750,000:
  Withholding = $112,500

## GOAT Scope Statement (use verbatim)
"Applies to all property sales unless the vendor provides a clearance certificate."

## Who It Applies To
Law targets foreign residents.
With $0 threshold, applies to ALL transactions by default.
Therefore:
  Australian residents must provide a clearance certificate — otherwise 15% is withheld.
  Foreign residents: withholding applies unless variation obtained.

## The Clearance Certificate
Form name: Foreign resident capital gains withholding clearance certificate application
Form number: NAT 74883
Apply via: ATO online portal (ato.gov.au)
Processing: Usually a few days
Allow up to: 28 days
Valid for: 12 months from issue date
Must be provided: to purchaser at settlement
Each vendor applies individually (joint vendors need separate certificates)

## The Consequence
Without certificate at settlement:
  Purchaser MUST withhold 15% of contract price
  Purchaser remits to ATO on vendor's behalf
  Vendor receives: purchase price minus 15%
  Buyer is legally liable if they fail to withhold

## Key Legal Caveats (accuracy-critical)
  Withholding = prepayment of tax NOT final liability
  Triggered at: contract date (not settlement date)
  Each vendor applies individually
  Certificate valid for 12 months from issue
  Buyer bears legal liability for failure to withhold

## The Deadline
Triggered at contract date.
Apply as early as possible — ideally when property is listed for sale.
Must be provided at settlement.
Processing up to 28 days so minimum lead time = 28 business days before settlement.
Recommend: apply when property hits the market.

## Common Misconceptions
MYTH: Only applies to foreign residents
FACT: From 1 Jan 2025, applies to ALL vendors by default unless certificate provided

MYTH: Only on investment properties
FACT: Applies to ALL real property sales including main residence

MYTH: Threshold is still $750,000
FACT: Changed to $0 from 1 January 2025

MYTH: It is a tax on profit
FACT: 15% of full contract price, not profit. A seller breaking even still has 15% withheld

MYTH: Buyer's conveyancer handles it
FACT: Each vendor must apply individually. Conveyancers often do not proactively advise
