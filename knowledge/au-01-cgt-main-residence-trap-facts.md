## The Rule

**Name:** Main Residence CGT Exemption — "Home First Used to Produce Income" Trap
**Short form:** Renting BEFORE you move in disqualifies the dwelling from the full main residence exemption forever. Moving in later does not undo the rental period.
**Operative law:** Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997), Division 118
- s.118-110 — General exemption for main residence
- s.118-145 — 6-year absence rule (only applies AFTER first occupied)
- s.118-185 — Partial exemption calculation
- s.118-190 — Reduction for income-producing use
- s.118-192 — Special rule: home first used to produce income (the trap)
- s.118-195 — Inherited dwelling exemption
**ATO landing page:** https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/capital-gains-tax/property-and-capital-gains-tax/your-main-residence-home

## The Threshold

**Effective date:** Original ITAA 1997 enactment. Section 118-192 applies to properties first used to produce income after 20 August 1996.
**Threshold:** Zero. There is no minimum rental period that triggers this rule. **One day of rental before moving in disqualifies the dwelling from the full exemption.**
**Rate:** Capital gains tax applies at the seller's marginal rate (up to 47% incl. Medicare levy) on the non-exempt portion, after the 50% CGT discount for individuals holding > 12 months from the deemed acquisition date.
**Base:** Market value of the dwelling on the day it first produced income (per s.118-192 cost base reset). NOT original purchase price.
**Previous threshold:** None. This rule has applied consistently since the s.118-192 amendment took effect 20 August 1996.

## The Fear Number

**Gary's worked example: $47,000.**

Gary bought a unit in Baldivis WA in 2018 for $480,000. He rented it for 6 years (2018-2024) at market rates. He moved in as his main residence in May 2024 and sold in May 2026 for $720,000.

- Total gain: $240,000
- Days owned: 2,920 (8 years)
- Days as income-producing (non-main-residence): 2,190 (6 years rental)
- Days as main residence: 730 (2 years)
- s.118-192 cost base reset: market value at first rental = $480,000 (deemed acquisition cost)
- Non-exempt fraction: 2,190 / 2,920 = 75%
- Taxable capital gain before discount: $240,000 × 75% = $180,000
- After 50% CGT discount: $90,000 added to taxable income
- Tax at 37% marginal rate (incl. Medicare levy): **~$33,300 to $47,000** depending on other income that year

## Who It Applies To

- Australian residents who rent a property before moving in as their main residence
- Investors who later occupy a property they previously rented out
- Deceased estates rented before being inherited and occupied
- Parents who buy property for adult children to occupy first
- Foreign residents are SEPARATELY ineligible for the main residence exemption entirely (different rule, see Caveats)
- Does NOT apply to dwellings that were main residence FIRST and rented out later (different rule under s.118-145, 6-year absence rule)

## The Form

**Tax return reporting:** Capital gains worksheet within the individual income tax return for the financial year of the contract sale date.
**Form name:** Capital gains tax (CGT) schedule (where required) — included as part of the standard individual income tax return.
**ATO calculator:** ATO Capital gains tax property exemption tool (online).
**Apply via:** myTax (online lodgement) or registered tax agent.
**Required records:** Original purchase contract, market valuation on date of first rental, market valuation on date moved in (recommended), all rental records, sale contract, capital improvement receipts.

## The Consequence

If the s.118-192 trap applies:
- No full exemption — ever
- Partial exemption only based on the days the dwelling was actually your main residence
- Cost base recalculated to market value on first occupation date
- 50% CGT discount available only if held > 12 months from the deemed acquisition date (when you first moved in), NOT from original purchase
- Taxable gain added to the year's assessable income at marginal rates (up to 47% incl. Medicare)
- The "6-year absence rule" under s.118-145 does NOT apply because the dwelling must have been your main residence FIRST

For a 6-year rental + 2-year occupation pattern: **~75% of the gain is taxable.**

## Key Legal Caveats

1. **Foreign residents:** If you sold while a foreign resident for tax purposes, you generally cannot claim the main residence exemption AT ALL. Foreign-resident exclusion took effect 1 July 2020 (subject to transitional rules for properties acquired before 9 May 2017).

2. **6-year absence rule does NOT save you.** The s.118-145 6-year rule only applies when the dwelling was your main residence BEFORE you started renting. Renting first then moving in disqualifies you from that rule entirely.

3. **Capital improvements:** Improvements made during rental period add to cost base. Improvements during main-residence period are partly attributable to exempt portion. Keep all receipts.

4. **Spousal main residence:** A married couple can only nominate ONE main residence between them at any time (with limited exceptions for relationship breakdown). If your spouse claimed another property as main residence during the period, you cannot claim this one.

5. **CGT discount eligibility:** The 12-month ownership period for the 50% CGT discount runs from the ORIGINAL acquisition date, NOT from the s.118-192 deemed acquisition date. The cost base resets under s.118-192 but the ownership period does not restart. Per ITAA 1997 Subdivision 115-A, the holding period is measured from when you first acquired the asset.

## The Deadline

**No fixed deadline** — the trap activates on every CGT event (sale, gift, transfer, deceased estate). The CGT event date is the contract sale date, NOT settlement date (per s.104-10).

For sellers currently caught:
- Lodge tax return for the financial year the contract was signed
- CGT event reportable in that year's return
- Standard ATO lodgement deadlines apply: 31 October if self-lodging, later if via registered tax agent

## Common Misconceptions

**MYTH:** "I lived in it for the last few years so it's fully exempt."
**FACT:** The s.118-192 "home first used to produce income" rule resets cost base to market value when you first moved in. The rental period before that is taxable.

**MYTH:** "The 6-year rule covers me — I can rent for up to 6 years and still claim exemption."
**FACT:** The s.118-145 6-year rule only applies if the dwelling was your main residence FIRST and you then moved out. Renting first does not qualify.

**MYTH:** "I'll just claim it was always my intention to live in it."
**FACT:** Intention is irrelevant. The ATO measures actual occupation. If a tenant lived there before you, the income-producing use clock has started.

**MYTH:** "The s.118-192 cost base reset also restarts my CGT discount holding period."
**FACT:** No. The cost base resets to market value at first income use, but the ownership period for the 12-month CGT discount runs from the original acquisition date per Subdivision 115-A. These are two separate rules — one resets the cost base, the other measures holding period.

**MYTH:** "I can use a current valuation to minimize tax."
**FACT:** Cost base is locked to market value on the day you first moved in. A current valuation is not relevant. Get a contemporaneous valuation if moving in to a previously-rented property.
