## The Rule

**Name:** Division 7A — Loans by Private Companies Treated as Deemed Dividends
**Short form:** A loan from your private company to you, your spouse, or your trust is treated as an unfranked dividend (taxable at your marginal rate, no franking credits) UNLESS it has a written complying loan agreement, charges at least the ATO benchmark interest rate, has a maximum 7-year term (25 years if secured by real property), and meets minimum yearly repayments by 30 June each year.
**Operative law:** Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (ITAA 1936), Part III, Division 7A
- s.109D — Loans treated as dividends
- s.109E — Amalgamated loans
- s.109N — Loans that satisfy the criteria for not being treated as dividends (the complying loan rules)
- s.109R — Repayments not taken into account in some circumstances (anti-circular-payment rule)
- s.109XA-XB — Unpaid present entitlements (UPE) provisions
**ATO landing page:** https://www.ato.gov.au/businesses-and-organisations/corporate-tax-measures-and-assurance/private-company-benefits-division-7a-dividends

## The Threshold

**Effective date:** Division 7A applies to loans made on or after 4 December 1997.
**Threshold:** Zero. Any unpaid balance from a private company to a shareholder or associate triggers Division 7A unless cured by 30 June of the year following the loan, OR put under a complying written agreement before the company's tax return lodgement day.
**Rate (benchmark interest):** **8.37% for 2025-26** (down from 8.77% in 2024-25). ATO sets this each year based on RBA's "Housing loans; Banks; Variable; Standard; Owner-occupier" rate published before 1 July of that year.
**Maximum loan term:** 7 years unsecured, 25 years secured by registered first mortgage over real property worth at least 110% of the loan amount.
**Lodgement-day deadline:** Written complying loan agreement must be in place before the private company's tax return lodgement day for the income year the loan was made.
**Minimum yearly repayment:** Must be paid by 30 June each year. Calculated using benchmark rate × prior-year balance × repayment factor formula.

## The Fear Number

**Gary's worked example: $94,000 deemed dividend tax on a $200,000 unintended loan.**

Gary's electrical contracting business (Mitchell Electrical Pty Ltd) paid $200,000 of Gary's personal expenses across the financial year — vehicle, family holiday, kitchen renovation, school fees. No loan agreement was put in place.

- Gary's accountant recognised the issue at year-end but the company tax return was due in 5 days
- No written complying loan agreement signed before lodgement day → s.109N exemption unavailable
- Full $200,000 treated as deemed unfranked dividend in Gary's name
- Added to Gary's assessable income at his 47% marginal rate (incl. Medicare levy)
- **Tax payable: ~$94,000**
- No franking credits available (Division 7A deems UNFRANKED dividends)
- Compare: a properly franked dividend would have given Gary $86,400 in franking credits

The trap closed because the agreement deadline passed. There is no late lodgement remedy under Division 7A.

## Who It Applies To

- Private company shareholders
- Associates of shareholders (spouses, family members, family trusts, partnerships in which they're partners)
- Beneficiaries of trusts where the trust has an Unpaid Present Entitlement (UPE) from a private company beneficiary
- Recipients of loans through interposed entities (s.109T anti-avoidance)
- Closely-held corporate limited partnerships (treated as private companies for Division 7A)
- Does NOT apply to genuine commercial loans between unrelated arms-length parties
- Does NOT apply to public companies or private companies that aren't presently entitled to retained profits

## The Form

**Tax return reporting:** Deemed dividends are reported as ordinary unfranked dividends on the shareholder's/associate's individual income tax return for the financial year the loan was made.
**Loan agreement form:** No prescribed ATO form. Must be a written agreement signed before lodgement day containing: loan amount, term, interest rate (≥ benchmark), security (if any), repayment schedule.
**ATO Division 7A calculator:** Available via ATO website to compute minimum yearly repayments.
**Apply via:** Loan agreement is private — kept on file by the company. Tax consequences flow through individual and company returns.
**Required records:** Written loan agreement, evidence of agreement timing (signed before lodgement day), bank statements showing repayments, board minutes authorising the loan.

## The Consequence

If Division 7A applies and no s.109N complying loan exemption exists:
- Full unpaid loan balance treated as unfranked deemed dividend in the year the loan was made
- Added to the recipient's assessable income at their marginal rate (up to 47% incl. Medicare)
- NO franking credits attached (this is the killer — properly declared franked dividends would carry franking credits)
- Cannot be reversed by repayment after the deadline
- ATO may issue amended assessments going back multiple years if the issue is detected in audit
- Penalties and General Interest Charge (GIC) apply on top

For complying loans that subsequently fail (missed repayment in a later year):
- Only the SHORTFALL is treated as deemed dividend, not the full balance
- Exception: if the entire loan term elapses without full repayment, the residual balance becomes deemed dividend

## Key Legal Caveats

1. **No verbal agreements.** Division 7A explicitly requires the loan agreement to be IN WRITING. A verbal understanding does not satisfy s.109N. The agreement must exist before the lodgement day for the income year the loan was made.

2. **Section 109R anti-circular rule:** If a shareholder repays a Division 7A loan and is then re-lent the same money shortly after, ATO may disregard the repayment under s.109R. Repayments must be "genuine and retained."

3. **Unpaid Present Entitlements (UPEs):** Per TD 2022/11 (and the 2024 Bendel Federal Court decision), UPEs from a trust to a corporate beneficiary may be treated as Division 7A loans if the funds are "accessed or used like a loan." Not all UPEs trigger Division 7A — depends on the arrangement.

4. **Lodgement day, not 30 June.** The complying loan agreement deadline is the company's tax return lodgement day (typically 28 February or later if via tax agent), NOT 30 June. The minimum yearly repayment deadline IS 30 June.

5. **Substituted accounting periods:** Companies with a substituted accounting period (not 1 July - 30 June) use a different RBA rate based on the rate published before THEIR income year start.

## The Deadline

**For loan agreements:** Lodgement day of the private company's income tax return for the year the loan was made (typically February-May the following year for standard 30 June year-ends with tax agent extensions).

**For minimum yearly repayments:** **30 June each income year.** Not 28 days later. Not when you lodge your return. By 30 June. Even one day late can convert the entire loan balance to a deemed dividend.

**For audit risk:** ATO can review Division 7A compliance going back up to 4 years (general amendment period) or 7 years (fraud or evasion). Some triggers extend to "unlimited" amendment periods.

## Common Misconceptions

**MYTH:** "I'll just put the loan agreement together at year-end and backdate it."
**FACT:** ATO scrutinises agreement dates. Backdating constitutes fraud. The agreement must genuinely exist before lodgement day.

**MYTH:** "If I repay the loan by 30 June, I'm fine."
**FACT:** Repayment by 30 June only saves you for that PARTICULAR income year. The original loan still triggered Division 7A at the time it was made. You need EITHER full repayment by lodgement day OR a complying loan agreement.

**MYTH:** "The benchmark rate is just a suggestion."
**FACT:** The benchmark rate is the MINIMUM. Charging less makes the loan non-complying — full balance becomes deemed dividend.

**MYTH:** "I can offset the loan against amounts the company owes me (e.g. unpaid wages or director fees)."
**FACT:** You cannot simply offset. The loan and the credit must be settled through proper accounting transactions, and the offset must occur within the relevant year.

**MYTH:** "Loans to my family trust are safe — Division 7A only applies to shareholders."
**FACT:** Division 7A captures associates of shareholders, including family trusts the shareholder controls. Trust loans are firmly in scope.
