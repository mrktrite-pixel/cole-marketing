## NEW REQUIRED FIELDS PER CHARACTER (added 2026-04-30)

Two fields are added to every character profile — one running, one
contrarian. Bees use them to power the bonus hook patterns documented
in G1 hook-matrix.md (RECEIPTS, STOLEN LESSON, MOST PEOPLE REVERSE).

### Field 1 — Story Vault
A running list of real moments, wins, near-misses for this character.
Each entry: `- [Date]: [1 sentence description]`. Bees pull from this
for Receipts Hooks (real counts, real dates) and Vulnerable Story Hooks
(specific moments).

Update this field each time a real case study or significant event is
noted — operator owns the cadence.

### Field 2 — Wedge (Viral Fuel)
The ONE contrarian belief this character holds — something most people
in their industry push back on. Used for the MOST PEOPLE REVERSE Hook
pattern and any Contrarian Take.

Format:
```
Most [audience] believe [X]. [Character] believes [Y] and has data to prove it.
```

Without a Wedge, the MOST PEOPLE REVERSE hook can't fire authentically
for that character's products. Operator defines the Wedge per character.

signs it. He has never really looked at the numbers.

The thing that got Gary to act was a conversation at
the fishing club. His mate Dave sold his rental property
and got hit with a tax bill he never saw coming. Dave
did not say the exact number but Gary could see it was
bad. That night Gary started googling. He did not like
what he found.

## THE VOICE CONSISTENCY CHECK

Before approving any content — run this check:

```
□ Read the first sentence out loud.
  Does it sound like a human or a brochure?

□ Is there a specific number in the first paragraph?
  Dollar amount, day count, percentage, or deadline?
  If not — add one before approving.

□ Check for banned phrases specific to this character.
  Even one banned phrase fails the check.
  Send back for rewrite.

□ Does the character acknowledge their own situation
  specifically? Not "people like you" — this person,
  this situation, this number.

□ Is the fear tangible?
  Not "you could owe tax" — "you could owe $47,000"
  Not "this might affect you" — "this will cost you
  $47,000 if the ATO calculates it the way they calculated
  it for Gary"

□ Would the character send this to a friend via text?
  If no — it is still too formal. Rewrite.
```
